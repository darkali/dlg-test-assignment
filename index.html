1. Web Development Workflow

Disability Law Group: Ensuring Accessibility, Security, and Growth

Focus: Zero-Downtime Reliability

PHASE 1: THE FOUNDATION (Maintenance & Security)

Focus: Zero-Downtime Reliability

  • Weekly Staging Updates: All Core/Plugin/Theme updates are tested in a sandbox before production.
  • Security Perimeter: Daily malware scans, Web Application Firewall (WAF) monitoring, and mandatory MFA.
  • Disaster Recovery: Automated daily off-site backups with monthly "Restore" verification.

PHASE 2: THE PERFORMANCE ENGINE (Speed & SEO)

Focus: Eliminating Technical Friction

  • Core Web Vitals Audit: Monthly tracking of LCP (Loading), FID (Interactivity), and CLS (Stability).
  • Asset Optimization: Converting images to WebP/AVIF and minifying CSS/JS scripts.
  • Schema Governance: Maintaining "LegalService" and "FAQ" Rich Snippets for search authority.

PHASE 3: THE COMPLIANCE PILLAR (Accessibility)

Focus: Inclusive Legal Representation

  • Automated Scanning: Bi-weekly WAVE/Axe audits to catch contrast and structural errors.
  • Manual UX Testing: Monthly screen reader walkthroughs (NVDA/VoiceOver) of lead-capture forms.
  • Standardization: Ensuring all new content meets WCAG 2.1 Level AA requirements.

PHASE 4: THE GROWTH CYCLE (Conversion & Content)

Focus: Maximizing Lead Generation

  • Campaign Deployment: Rapid-sprint landing page creation for Social Security/VA campaigns.
  • Conversion Optimization: A/B testing CTA placements and monitoring form abandonment rates.
  • Link Integrity: Weekly 404 monitoring and internal linking audits to preserve domain authority.

PHASE 5: THE DEPLOYMENT PIPELINE (CI/CD)

Focus: Quality Assurance

  1. Development: Feature coding and local testing.
  2. Staging: Partner review and approval.
  3. QA Audit: Final speed and accessibility certification.
  4. Launch: Production deployment during low-traffic windows.
  5. Post-Launch: Immediate verification of conversion tracking (GA4/GTM).

2. SEO Expansion Plan

To move from a local Michigan firm to a statewide and national authority, the SEO strategy must shift from geography-based keywords to intent-based and authority-based content. Because Social Security Disability (SSD) and Veterans (VA) law are federal, the firm is uniquely positioned to capture national traffic by demonstrating E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness).

Strategic Objective: Establish Disability Law Group as the primary digital resource for SSD and VA law in Michigan while capturing high-intent national search traffic through topical authority.

1. Statewide Dominance (Michigan Saturation)

  • Geographic Landing Pages (LPs): Create "Local Hubs" for major Michigan cities (e.g., Detroit, Grand Rapids, Lansing, Flint, Ann Arbor). These are not duplicate pages; they should include:
    • Specific local court information and regional SSD office addresses.
    • Localized testimonials and success stories.
    • Local "Legal Resources" sections to build community relevance.
  • Hyper-Local Schema Markup (JSON-LD): Implement AreaServed and LocalBusiness schema for each targeted region. This helps Google’s "Map Pack" and localized search results understand the firm’s service radius.
  • Google Business Profile (GBP) Expansion: If the firm has satellite offices, optimize each GBP with localized posts, city-specific photos, and a localized review acquisition strategy.

2. National Authority (Topical Content Hubs)

  • The "Topic Cluster" Model: Create central "Pillar Pages" for broad topics (e.g., "The Complete Guide to VA Disability Ratings").
    • Sub-topics: Link the pillar to 10+ smaller, specific articles (e.g., "VA Ratings for Tinnitus," "How to Increase a 70% Rating").
    • Internal Linking: Use a "Silo" structure to pass link equity from the high-traffic pillar page down to specific conversion-focused articles.
  • Evergreen "Federal Law" Resources: Develop tools that users nationwide search for, such as:
    • An SSD Benefits Calculator or a VA Disability Rating Calculator.
    • A downloadable "Appeals Timeline" infographic.
    • Interactive "Checklists" for medical evidence requirements.
  • E-E-A-T Optimization:
    • Author Bylines: Every legal article must be attributed to a specific attorney with a link to their bio.
    • Editorial Review: Add "Fact-Checked by [Attorney Name]" on all medical/legal guides.
    • Cite Sources: Link to official government sources (.gov) like the SSA or VA.gov to boost trust signals.

3. Technical SEO & Accessibility (The Developer’s Edge)

  • Accessibility as SEO: Search engines reward sites that are easy to crawl and use.
    • Ensuring 100% semantic HTML (proper <h1-h6> nesting) helps Google understand content hierarchy.
    • Alt-text for all images provides context for both screen readers and Google Image Search.
  • Core Web Vitals (CWV) Optimization:
    • Prioritize Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) by optimizing the "hero" images on landing pages.
    • Minimize Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS) by pre-defining space for images and ads to ensure a stable reading experience for users with motor disabilities.
  • Mobile-First Architecture: Ensure the site is "thumb-friendly" and fast-loading on 4G connections, as many veterans in rural areas rely on mobile data.

4. Off-Page Authority & Link Building

  • Legal Directory Saturation: Ensure "Clean" and consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) data across Avvo, Justia, Martindale-Hubbell, and FindLaw.
  • Strategic Outreach:
    • Partner with Veteran Service Organizations (VSOs) and non-profits to provide educational guest content in exchange for a backlink.
    • Seek "Resource Page" links from Michigan-based disability support groups.
  • Digital PR: Monitor platforms like "Featured" (formerly HARO) for journalists looking for legal experts on Social Security or Veterans affairs. One mention in a national outlet (e.g., Forbes, USA Today) can skyrocket domain authority.

3. Analytics and Reporting Strategy

For a marketing-driven legal practice like the Disability Law Group, an analytics framework must bridge the gap between technical web performance and business-critical lead generation. The following strategy focuses on capturing high-intent behaviors while providing the transparency needed to optimize marketing spend.

1. User Behaviors & Business Events

Tracking is divided into Macro-Conversions (direct revenue-driving actions) and Micro-Conversions (indicators of intent and brand trust).

Macro-Conversions (Business Events)

  • Lead Form Submissions: Successful completion of "Free Case Evaluation" or "Contact Us" forms.
  • Click-to-Call: Phone number clicks from mobile and desktop users.
  • Live Chat Initiation: When a user starts a conversation with the site’s intake bot or representative.
  • Email Link Clicks (Mailto): Direct clicks to the firm’s contact emails.

Micro-Conversions (Engagement Behaviors)

  • Resource Downloads: Tracking when users download "VA Rating Checklists" or "SSD Appeal Guides."
  • Video Engagement: Monitoring play rates and 75% completion marks on educational videos regarding disability law.
  • Scroll Depth: Measuring users who reach the bottom of long-form legal articles (indicating high topical interest).
  • Internal Search Queries: Tracking what users search for within the site to identify gaps in educational content.
  • Accessibility Interactions: Tracking use of accessibility widgets or high-contrast toggles to ensure the site meets the needs of its core demographic.

2. Event Tracking Structure (GA4 & GTM)

To maintain data integrity and allow for easy scaling, a standardized naming convention is essential. Using Google Tag Manager (GTM) to push events into Google Analytics 4 (GA4) ensures that the tracking is decoupled from the hard code.

Event Tracking Structure for GA4 & GTM
Event Name Parameter: category Parameter: label Parameter: value
generate_lead form_submission footer_evaluation_form (Assigned Lead Value)
contact_click click_to_call header_phone_number 1
file_download educational_resource va_rating_guide_pdf 0
video_progress educational_video how_to_file_ssd_claim % Watched

Implementation Detail: We will utilize a Data Layer implementation for form submissions. Instead of just tracking a button click (which can lead to false positives), we will trigger the event only upon a successful "thank you" state or server-side validation.

3. Key Metrics for Performance Reports

  • Conversion Rate by Source/Medium: Identifying which channels (Organic Search, PPC, Social) provide the highest quality leads.
  • Cost Per Lead (CPL): Total marketing spend divided by total macro-conversions.
  • Landing Page Attribution: Identifying which specific legal guides or blog posts are the "First Touch" that eventually leads to a consultation.
  • Site Stability & Accessibility Score: Monthly reporting on Core Web Vitals (LCP, CLS) and WCAG compliance levels to ensure the site isn't creating barriers for claimants.
  • Form Abandonment Rate: Identifying specific fields (e.g., asking for a Social Security Number too early) that cause users to drop off before submitting.

4. Informing Strategic Decision-Making

  • Content Strategy: If the "VA Disability" guides have a high scroll depth but low conversion, we know we need to optimize the Call-to-Action (CTA) placement on those specific pages.
  • Budget Allocation: If Paid Search (PPC) shows a high volume of clicks but a low conversion rate compared to Organic SEO, the firm can pivot budget toward high-authority content creation.
  • UX Improvements: If the "Contact Us" form has a high abandonment rate on mobile, we can prioritize a technical redesign of the mobile intake interface.
